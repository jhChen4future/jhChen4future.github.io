# Research Bottleneck Taxonomy

Use this reference when the visible request may not be the true constraint.

## 1. Question bottlenecks
- Scope is too broad to yield a discriminating contribution.
- Outcome or mechanism is not clearly defined.
- The question is descriptive while the claimed contribution is causal.
- The project asks what happens but not relative to what counterfactual.

## 2. Conceptual bottlenecks
- Constructs overlap or change meaning across fields.
- A behavior is treated as a preference, belief, norm, or mechanism without identification.
- The proposed causal chain skips necessary intermediate steps.
- A taxonomy is convenient but not theoretically grounded.

## 3. Evidence bottlenecks
- Central claims rely on secondary summaries.
- Evidence is abundant but non-diagnostic.
- Contradictory findings are explained away rather than modeled.
- The evidence base is restricted by keywords, disciplines, languages, or publication bias.

## 4. Identification bottlenecks
- Treatment is endogenous or bundled.
- The estimand does not match the verbal claim.
- Controls introduce post-treatment bias or bad-control problems.
- Parallel trends, exclusion restrictions, SUTVA, monotonicity, or functional form are unsupported.
- Robust standard errors are used as a substitute for identification.

## 5. Measurement bottlenecks
- A proxy has weak construct validity.
- A task jointly measures multiple latent traits.
- Self-report and behavior are treated as interchangeable.
- Reliability, scale invariance, ceiling/floor effects, or comprehension are untested.

## 6. Data bottlenecks
- Sample size, selection, attrition, or missingness limits inference.
- Variable construction is opaque or unstable.
- Data coverage does not match the target population or time horizon.
- Data cleaning choices drive results.

## 7. Model bottlenecks
- Model complexity exceeds what the data identify.
- The outcome distribution or dependence structure is misspecified.
- Results depend on one arbitrary specification.
- A prediction model is interpreted structurally or causally.

## 8. Experimental-design bottlenecks
- Treatment manipulation is not isolated.
- Incentives, beliefs, strategic pressure, or experimenter demand contaminate the construct.
- Randomization, balance, power, or implementation integrity is weak.
- Mechanism tests are merely additional correlations.

## 9. Computational bottlenecks
- Results are not reproducible from raw inputs.
- Code paths diverge across analyses.
- Silent data coercion, merges, lags, indexing, or seed handling introduce errors.
- No unit, integration, or invariance checks exist.

## 10. Interpretation bottlenecks
- Statistical significance is treated as substantive importance.
- Null results are treated as proof of no effect.
- Heterogeneity is post hoc and underpowered.
- The conclusion exceeds the design's scope.

## 11. Workflow bottlenecks
- The project repeatedly collects information without decision rules.
- Tasks are completed in presentation order rather than dependency order.
- Old outputs dominate context and anchor later work.
- No stable state record exists.
- The next action is selected because it is easy, not informative.

## Selecting the bottleneck
Score candidate bottlenecks on:
1. Threat to validity
2. Downstream leverage
3. Resolvability
4. Expected information gain
5. Cost

Choose the highest-leverage resolvable bottleneck, not necessarily the highest-severity one.
