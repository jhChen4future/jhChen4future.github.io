# Experiments and Measurement Operations

## Decompose the observation
Observed behavior may reflect:
- preferences;
- beliefs;
- risk or ambiguity attitudes;
- strategic incentives;
- comprehension;
- social norms;
- experimenter demand;
- learning;
- attention;
- constraints;
- noise.

## Design branches
- Construct-validity audit
- Manipulation check and comprehension check
- Incentive compatibility
- Strategic-pressure decomposition
- Belief elicitation
- Alternative task triangulation
- Within- versus between-subject design
- Power and minimum detectable effect
- Treatment spillover and contamination
- Pre-analysis and stopping rules

## Mechanism standard
A mediator regression alone rarely identifies a mechanism. Prefer design variation that shifts the proposed mechanism while holding rival pathways stable, or state clearly that evidence is suggestive.

## Progress artifact
Leave a design table, construct-to-measure map, threat matrix, power calculation, or revised protocol.
